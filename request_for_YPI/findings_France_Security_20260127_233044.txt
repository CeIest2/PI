### QUESTION: **Q4 [GOOGLE-SEARCH]:** What are the current and upcoming national initiatives or government-led projects in France related to digital infrastructure development, cybersecurity, or internet resilience?
ANSWER:
France is actively engaged in national initiatives and government-led projects focused on digital infrastructure development, cybersecurity, and internet resilience, with a particular emphasis on aligning with the European Digital Decade objectives and fostering technological advancement.

A key overarching strategy is the **National Digital Decade strategic roadmap**, developed by the Government of France. This roadmap sets out strategic goals for 2030, monitors their achievement, and outlines target paths and key measures. It is structured around four main chapters: Digital Skills, Digital Infrastructures, Digital Transformation of Businesses, and Digitalisation of Public Services [Source 2].

Within this framework, several initiatives are underway:

*   **Digital Infrastructure Development:** While not explicitly detailed in the provided findings, the "Digital Infrastructures" chapter of the National Digital Decade roadmap indicates a focus on this area. Cisco's recent announcement of establishing a **Global AI Hub** in France, which includes a focus on "secure, energy-efficient AI infrastructure" and a global center of expertise for innovative cooling solutions for data centers, aligns with and supports this objective. This initiative is explicitly stated to align with **France 2030**, the national strategy for technological advancement and economic resilience [Source 4].

*   **Cybersecurity:** Cybersecurity is a prominent component of France's digital strategy. Cisco's Country Digital Acceleration (CDA) program has driven national-scale projects in France, including those related to **cybersecurity** [Source 4]. Furthermore, Cisco's digital skills training programs, delivered through its Networking Academy, offer free courses that enhance digital literacy with a focus on **cybersecurity**, alongside other critical areas like data science, IoT, and AI [Source 4].

*   **Digital Skills and Workforce Development:** A significant portion of France's national initiatives is dedicated to enhancing digital skills. The **Skills and Jobs of the Future initiative** has a budget of EUR 2.5 billion and aims to meet employment and skills needs, accelerating training related to national Research and Development strategies. This initiative focuses on training in upper secondary schools, higher education, and lifelong learning, guided by the France 2030 strategy [Source 2]. Cisco plans to train 230,000 people in France over the next three years through its Networking Academy, offering programs from basic skills to professional certifications, with a focus on digital awareness, cybersecurity, data science, IoT, and AI [Source 4]. These efforts are crucial for building a digitally skilled workforce capable of supporting advanced digital infrastructures and cybersecurity measures.

*   **Digital Preservation:** While the findings from 2003 are dated, they highlight France's historical engagement with national digital preservation initiatives. The report from CLIR indicates that France was surveyed as part of a study on national digital preservation initiatives, suggesting a recognized need for strategies to ensure the long-term archiving of digital content [Source 1, Source 3]. Although specific current projects in this area are not detailed in the provided findings, the broader context of digital infrastructure development and resilience would implicitly encompass the need for robust digital preservation strategies.

In summary, France's current and upcoming national initiatives are strongly driven by its **National Digital Decade strategic roadmap** and the **France 2030** plan. These efforts encompass building secure and energy-efficient digital infrastructures, enhancing cybersecurity capabilities through training and strategic projects, and significantly investing in digital skills development to prepare its workforce for the future of technology.
