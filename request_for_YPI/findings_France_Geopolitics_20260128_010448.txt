Here's a breakdown of what the findings reveal and what is missing:

**What the Findings Address (Globally, with implications for France):**

*   **Challenges in Identifying State-Owned ASNs:** Sources [1] and [3] extensively discuss the difficulties in accurately identifying state-owned Internet operators and their ASNs. These challenges include:
    *   **Lack of Public Databases:** There is no universal, public registry for state-owned enterprises, and national reports are often not granular enough [1, 3].
    *   **Inaccurate Commercial Databases:** Databases like Orbis and Telegeography's GlobalComms may not be entirely accurate or comprehensive, with undisclosed methodologies and update frequencies [1, 3].
    *   **Company-to-ASN Mapping Issues:** WHOIS records can be outdated, legal names may differ from brand names, and sibling ASNs (multiple ASNs under one company) can have different identifiers [1, 3].
    *   **Complex Ownership Structures:** State ownership can be indirect through funds or chains of companies, and subsidiaries abroad may report ownership inconsistently [1, 3]. Ownership is also dynamic due to privatizations and re-nationalizations [1, 3].
    *   **Data Scarcity:** Obtaining this information on a global scale is a significant challenge [1, 3].
    *   **Defining "State-Owned":** Different criteria exist (e.g., IMF, OECD, European Commission), with the paper adopting the IMF definition of >= 50% equity [3].

*   **Potential Implications of State Ownership:** The papers highlight that understanding state ownership is crucial for studies related to Internet censorship, surveillance, cyber-warfare, and critical infrastructure protection [1, 3].

*   **Role of Regulators and Registries:** The findings mention that Regional Internet Registries (RIRs) provide WHOIS data, but it can be outdated. US regulators like the SEC and FCC may have ownership information for companies with US operations. Local regulators might also disclose domestic company details [1, 3]. AFNIC is mentioned in relation to local expert validation [3].
