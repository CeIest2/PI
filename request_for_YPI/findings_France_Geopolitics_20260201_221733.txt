### QUESTION: **Q4 [GOOGLE-SEARCH]:** What are the current and upcoming national initiatives or government-backed projects in France related to digital infrastructure development, cybersecurity, or internet resilience?
ANSWER:
France is actively engaged in developing its digital infrastructure, enhancing cybersecurity, and bolstering internet resilience through a combination of national initiatives and government-backed projects, often aligned with broader European Union strategies.

**Key Current and Upcoming Initiatives:**

*   **France 2030:** This overarching €54 billion investment initiative is a cornerstone of France's strategy to boost key sectors and foster emerging technologies, with a significant focus on digital security and startups [Source 1]. It aims to support the development of advanced digital skills and the implementation of training related to national Research and Development strategies in key digital sectors [Source 3]. Specifically, France 2030 is investing up to €735 million by 2025 to support 5G and future network technologies, with a total leverage of €1.7 billion [Source 1].

*   **National Cyber Security Strategy:** France has a robust national cyber security strategy, initially adopted in 2015 and subsequently supplemented by international digital strategy and a strategic review of cyber defense. This strategy aims to guarantee national sovereignty, provide a strong response to cybercrime, inform the public, make digital security a competitive advantage for French businesses, and enhance France's international standing in cybersecurity [Source 4]. The French Network and Information Security Agency (ANSSI) is the national cyber security authority, responsible for preventing and reacting to IT incidents and organizing national crisis exercises [Source 4].

*   **NIS2 Directive Implementation:** As mandated by the EU, France is in the process of implementing the NIS2 Directive, with implementation expected in Fall 2024. This directive aims to significantly enhance cybersecurity for critical infrastructures and digital service providers across the EU, imposing stricter obligations for cybersecurity measures and incident reporting [Source 1].

*   **SecNumCloud and Trusted Cloud Strategy:** France, through ANSSI, maintains its own security certification scheme for cloud services called SecNumCloud. The French Government's Trusted Cloud Strategy, issued in May 2021, mandates SecNumCloud certification for government agencies and critical commercial entities handling highly sensitive data. This initiative aims to ensure the security and sovereignty of cloud computing services used by critical entities [Source 1].

*   **AI Act Implementation:** The EU's AI Act, proposed in 2021, came into force in August 2024. France is adhering to this regulation, which provides a risk-based approach to regulating artificial intelligence [Source 1].

*   **Digital Decade Strategic Roadmap:** France has developed a strategic roadmap for the Digital Decade, outlining goals to be achieved by 2030. This roadmap focuses on four key areas: Digital Skills, Digital Infrastructures, Digital Transformation of Businesses, and Digitalisation of Public Services [Source 3].

*   **Digital Commons Initiative:** France has been a driving force behind the concept of "Digital Commons" within the EU, advocating for the establishment of sovereign digital infrastructures that leverage digital networks in a non-hegemonic way. A report on Digital Commons, supported by 17 EU Member States, calls for measures such as establishing a European foundation for digital commons to facilitate collaboration between digital commons ecosystem actors and public authorities [Source 5]. This initiative is seen as crucial for strengthening European digital sovereignty by challenging enclosure strategies and promoting multilateral governance of data and digital tools [Source 5].

*   **Cisco's Strategic Initiatives:** Cisco has announced significant strategic initiatives in France, including the creation of a Global AI Hub focused on secure, energy-efficient AI infrastructure, digital upskilling, and startup ecosystem innovation. This aligns with the France 2030 strategy [Source 2]. Cisco plans to train 230,000 people in France over the next three years in AI and digital skills, building on its existing commitment to digital transformation and its Country Digital Acceleration (CDA) program, which has driven 14 national-scale projects in areas like cybersecurity and sovereign cloud since 2015 [Source 2]. Cisco is also investing in the French startup ecosystem, including Mistral AI, and is deepening research and educational partnerships with French universities, focusing on AI and Quantum Technologies [Source 2].

These initiatives collectively demonstrate France's commitment to building a secure, resilient, and innovative digital future, with a strong emphasis on national sovereignty, economic competitiveness, and alignment with European digital ambitions.
